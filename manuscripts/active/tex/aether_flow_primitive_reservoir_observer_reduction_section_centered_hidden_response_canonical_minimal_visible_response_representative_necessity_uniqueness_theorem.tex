The next primary manuscript must therefore derive or force that current visible-response datum itself from still more primitive explicit substrate structure. Another same-output deeper relay or a re-expansion back to the full current image-core presentation, the larger reduced-carrier presentation, the older projection/body presentation, or the full displacement-response substrate law is no longer the right front-line move.

\section{Main Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Canonical minimal visible-response representative necessity / uniqueness on the current route]
\label{thm:main}
For every visible-response-faithful benchmark reduction \(\VisibleReduction\) of the current section-centered hidden-response route, the canonical minimal visible-response representative \(\MinimalReducedStructure_{\sigma}\) of Definition \ref{def:route}(5) is necessary and unique at benchmark scope up to visible-response-faithful equivalence. More precisely:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item every minimal observer-relevant visible-response datum \(\VisibleDatum(\VisibleReduction)\) must determine both the section-centered displacement-body datum and the positive visible-response operator on the current route;
    \item \(\MinimalReducedStructure_{\sigma}\) itself is sufficient to determine every benchmark-facing derivation statement available on that route;
    \item any two minimal observer-relevant visible-response data are visible-response-faithfully equivalent to \(\MinimalReducedStructure_{\sigma}\), and hence to each other.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Item (1) is the combined content of Propositions \ref{prop:body} and \ref{prop:operator}. Those propositions show that no minimal observer-relevant datum on the current route may omit either the displacement-body constituent or the visible-response-operator constituent.

Item (2) is Proposition \ref{prop:sufficient}. The earlier current-image-core collapse theorem already proves that all current benchmark-facing downstream uses factor through \(\MinimalReducedStructure_{\sigma}\).

For item (3), let \(\VisibleDatum^{(1)}\) and \(\VisibleDatum^{(2)}\) be two minimal observer-relevant visible-response data for visible-response-faithful benchmark reductions of the current route. By item (1), each must determine the same kind of displacement-body and visible-response-operator constituents through which the route recovers the current body-response core and the benchmark-facing downstream package. By item (2), neither needs anything beyond those constituents at current benchmark scope. Therefore each is visible-response-faithfully equivalent to \(\MinimalReducedStructure_{\sigma}\) in the sense of Definition \ref{def:equiv}. Hence they are visible-response-faithfully equivalent to each other as well.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[No smaller benchmark-facing datum remains on the current route]
\label{cor:minimal}
On the current section-centered hidden-response route, there is no genuinely smaller benchmark-facing datum below \(\MinimalReducedStructure_{\sigma}\) up to visible-response-faithful equivalence.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If a smaller datum existed, it would either omit the displacement-body constituent or the visible-response-operator constituent proved necessary by Propositions \ref{prop:body} and \ref{prop:operator}, or else merely rename the same constituents and therefore remain visible-response-faithfully equivalent to \(\MinimalReducedStructure_{\sigma}\).
