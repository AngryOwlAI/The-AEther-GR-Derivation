\begin{abstract}
The current active primitive-reservoir observer-reduction frontier already moved the live burden below the proto-germ package: the present proto-germ package was derived from a more primitive proto-germ-generating substrate pre-proto-germ dynamics package. The present note takes that route. It isolates the \emph{observer-relevant pre-proto-germ core}: the pre-proto-germ state space and compact body, the projection to the recorded proto-germ body together with its proto section, the pre-proto-germ restriction section and exact shell, the embedded zero core and split, the hidden charge, the observer-compatible exact zero-response realization, and the zero-core solvability / coercive package. Any benchmark-faithful completion of the same observer-relevant core is benchmark-equivalent, and a canonical completion is obtained by composing the pre-proto proto minimizer with the already fixed downstream proto-germ lower minimizers.

The benchmark boundary remains fixed throughout. The one operative metric is still exactly \(\CompletedMetric\), the ordinary matter route is unchanged, there is no second operative metric, and the low-energy matter law is not enlarged. The positive result is therefore narrower than a completed first-principles derivation and sharper than another same-output relay: the live pre-proto-germ burden now collapses to a smaller observer-relevant core. What remains open is to derive or justify that smaller core from still more primitive substrate structure, or else prove a still sharper collapse strictly inside it.

\begin{proposition}[Canonical completion of the omitted lower ladder]
\label{prop:canonical}
Fix an observer-relevant pre-proto-germ core \(\PreProtoCore\). Then there is a canonical benchmark-faithful completion obtained by
\[
\widehat{\PreProtoGermMin{\sigma}}
\equiv
\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\circ\ProtoGermMin{\sigma},
\qquad
\widehat{\PreProtoSeedMin{\sigma}}
\equiv
\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\circ\ProtoSeedMin{\sigma},
\]
\[
\widehat{\PreProtoFoundMin{\sigma}}
\equiv
\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\circ\ProtoFoundMin{\sigma},
\qquad
\widehat{\PreProtoGroundMin{\sigma}}
\equiv
\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\circ\ProtoGroundMin{\sigma},
\]
\[
\widehat{\PreProtoOrigMin{\sigma}}
\equiv
\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\circ\ProtoOrigMin{\sigma},
\qquad
\widehat{\PreProtoPrecMin{\sigma}}
\equiv
\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\circ\ProtoPrecMin{\sigma},
\]
\[
\widehat{\PreProtoLiftMin{\sigma}}
\equiv
\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\circ\ProtoLiftMin{\sigma},
\qquad
\widehat{\PreProtoSubMin{\sigma}}
\equiv
\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\circ\ProtoSubMin{\sigma}.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Each displayed composition has the correct codomain \(\PreProtoBody{\sigma}\) because \(\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\) maps \(\ProtoBody{\sigma}\) into \(\PreProtoBody{\sigma}\). Using
\[
\PreProtoProj{\sigma}\circ\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}
=
\mathrm{id}_{\ProtoBody{\sigma}}
\]
from Definition \ref{def:core}(1), we obtain immediately
\[
\PreProtoProj{\sigma}\circ\widehat{\PreProtoGermMin{\sigma}}
=
\ProtoGermMin{\sigma},
\qquad
\PreProtoProj{\sigma}\circ\widehat{\PreProtoSeedMin{\sigma}}
=
\ProtoSeedMin{\sigma},
\]
and likewise for the foundation, ground, origin, precursor, lift, and substrate maps. All other data required by Definition \ref{def:preproto} are already part of the core. Therefore the displayed compositions provide a benchmark-faithful completion.
\end{proof}

    \item and the same benchmark outputs \eqref{eq:fixedoutputs}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Core-equivalent completions are benchmark-equivalent]
\label{prop:equiv}
Any two core-equivalent benchmark-faithful completions are benchmark-equivalent in the sense of Definition \ref{def:bencheq}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By definition, core-equivalent completions agree on the projection \(\PreProtoProj{\sigma}\), the proto section \(\PreProtoProtoMin{\sigma}\), the restriction section \(\PreProtoSection{\sigma}\), the exact shell \(\PreProtoShellSet{\sigma}\), the zero core \(\PreProtoZeroCore{\sigma}\), the split \(\PreProtoSplit{\sigma}\), the hidden charge \(\PreProtoCharge{\sigma}\), the exact zero-response realization \(\PreProtoEmbed{\sigma}\), the kernel chart \(\PreProtoKernelCh{\sigma}\), the orthogonal projector \(\PreProtoStateComp{\sigma}\), and the benchmark-faithfulness condition. Those are exactly the data that determine the seven items listed in Definition \ref{def:bencheq}. The omitted lower minimizer ladder is not used in any of those determinations. Therefore every pair of core-equivalent completions is benchmark-equivalent.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[The live proto-germ frontier depends only on the observer-relevant core]
\label{prop:frontier}
Fix an observer-relevant pre-proto-germ core. Then every benchmark-faithful completion of that core induces the same observer-relevant proto-germ frontier data and therefore the same downstream benchmark-facing consequences already recorded on the active primitive-reservoir line.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By Proposition \ref{prop:equiv}, every benchmark-faithful completion of the fixed core induces the same projected exact shell, zero core, split, hidden charge, exact zero-response realization, fixed-data kernel package, and benchmark outputs. These are precisely the pre-proto-to-proto transport data that matter observer-relevantly for the current proto-germ frontier and the fixed benchmark one-metric package. Since the omitted lower minimizer ladder does not alter those data, it does not alter the observer-relevant proto-germ frontier or any downstream benchmark-facing consequence already recorded from that frontier.
\end{proof}

\section{Main Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-relevant pre-proto-germ core collapse]
\label{thm:main}
Fix the primitive continuation data of Definition \ref{def:fixed}, the recorded downstream ladder and proto-germ target of Definition \ref{def:proto_target}, and a benchmark-faithful full pre-proto-germ package in the sense of Definition \ref{def:preproto}. Then, for each sector \(\sigma\in\{\Car,\Cur,\Hom\}\), the live benchmark-facing burden inside that full pre-proto-germ package collapses to the observer-relevant core of Definition \ref{def:core}. More precisely:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item the omitted lower minimizer ladder
    \[
    \begin{aligned}
    \PreProtoGermMin{\sigma},\quad
    \PreProtoSeedMin{\sigma},\quad
    \PreProtoFoundMin{\sigma},\quad
    \PreProtoGroundMin{\sigma},\\
    \PreProtoOrigMin{\sigma},\quad
    \PreProtoPrecMin{\sigma},\quad
    \PreProtoLiftMin{\sigma},\quad
    \PreProtoSubMin{\sigma}
    \end{aligned}
    \]
    is not an independent benchmark-facing burden at current scope;
    \item every observer-relevant pre-proto-germ core admits a canonical benchmark-faithful completion by Proposition \ref{prop:canonical};
    \item any two benchmark-faithful completions of the same observer-relevant core are benchmark-equivalent and induce the same current proto-germ-frontier data by Propositions \ref{prop:equiv} and \ref{prop:frontier};
    \item the benchmark one-metric package remains fixed: the operative metric is still exactly \(\CompletedMetric\), the ordinary matter route is unchanged, there is no second operative metric, and the low-energy matter law is not enlarged;
    \item consequently the remaining honest burden below the current proto-germ-frontier theorem is no longer the full pre-proto-germ package as such, and still less another same-output deeper-origin relay that leaves this core unchanged. What remains is to derive or justify the observer-relevant pre-proto-germ core itself from still more primitive substrate structure, or else prove a still sharper collapse strictly inside that core.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Item (1) is the content of Propositions \ref{prop:equiv} and \ref{prop:frontier}: once the observer-relevant core is fixed, varying the omitted lower minimizer ladder does not change any benchmark-facing output. Item (2) is Proposition \ref{prop:canonical}. Item (3) is the combination of Propositions \ref{prop:equiv} and \ref{prop:frontier}. Item (4) is part of benchmark faithfulness and the fixed outputs \eqref{eq:fixedoutputs}. Item (5) is the routing consequence of the previous items together with the benchmark compression safeguard already fixed on the active line.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[What remains open after the core collapse]
\label{cor:next}
After Theorem \ref{thm:main}, the remaining honest burden is no longer to treat the full pre-proto-germ lower minimizer ladder as primitive benchmark-facing data. That burden has collapsed to the observer-relevant pre-proto-germ core. What remains open is why that smaller core should hold, or whether an even sharper theorem can collapse that core further without changing the same benchmark one-metric package and claim boundary.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Theorem \ref{thm:main} shows that the omitted lower ladder is benchmark-neutral once the observer-relevant core is fixed. Therefore the next honest open burden lies at the level of that core or below it, not at the level of another same-output relay or another full-package restatement.
\end{proof}

\section{Conclusion}

The positive result of this note is narrower than a completed first-principles derivation and sharper than another same-output pre-proto-germ relay. The current frontier had already moved the active burden below the proto-germ package by deriving that package from a more primitive pre-proto-germ class. The present theorem then shows that, inside that current pre-proto-germ class itself, the live benchmark-facing burden is smaller than the full recorded package: the omitted lower minimizer ladder is not an independent observer-relevant burden once the pre-proto-germ core is fixed.

The scope remains disciplined. The benchmark package is unchanged: the one operative metric is still exactly \(\CompletedMetric\), the ordinary matter route is unchanged, there is no second operative metric, and the low-energy matter law is not enlarged. Nothing here upgrades adoption to completed derivation, and nothing here licenses stronger promotion language.

The open burden is therefore cleaner. The next benchmark-facing manuscript should derive or justify the observer-relevant pre-proto-germ core from still more primitive substrate structure, unless the sharper honest gain available instead is a further theorem-level collapse strictly inside that core. Until then, the present note should be read for what it is: a genuine internal compression of the active pre-proto-germ frontier in \TheoryName{}, not a claim that the first-principles program is finished.
