\begin{proof}

\[

=
\ker\HiddenSel{\sigma}\oplus \operatorname{im}\HiddenSel{\sigma}

\ZeroHiddenVec{\sigma}\oplus\GapSpace{\sigma},

\[
\GapSpace{\sigma}
=
{\ZeroHiddenVec{\sigma}}^{\perp}\subset\BalSpace{\sigma},
\]

\[
\ev{\eta,\BalOp{\sigma}\eta}
=
\ev{\eta,\HiddenOperator{\sigma}\eta}
\ge
\kappa_{\sigma}^{\mathrm{hid}}\|\eta\|^{2},
\]
which is \eqref{eq:ambientgap}. The hidden projection body and the exact body splitting are exactly the hidden body \(\GapBody{\sigma}\) and the selector-level splitting \eqref{eq:gensplitting}. The shell realization and data compatibility are exactly \eqref{eq:gencompat}, because
\[
\BalEmbed{\sigma}(\ForSpace{\sigma})
=
\GenBody{\sigma}\cap\ker\HiddenSel{\sigma}\cap(\GenShell{\sigma})^{-1}(0)
=
\BalBody{\sigma}\cap\ZeroHiddenSlice{\sigma}\cap(\BalShell{\sigma})^{-1}(0).
\]

\section{Forced Constituents and Uniqueness}

\begin{proposition}[All ambient constituents are forced by the selector law]

Item (4) is exactly the selector image together with the hidden body and operator already present in Definition \ref{def:generator}. Item (5) follows because the ambient balance operator must vanish exactly on selector-zero directions and reproduce the hidden-sector quadratic form on selector-image directions, which is precisely the action of \(\BalOp{\sigma}(v)=\HiddenOperator{\sigma}(\HiddenSel{\sigma}v)\). Item (6) is the content of Proposition \ref{prop:ambient}: all those later ambient constituents descend directly from the same selector package and carry no additional independent freedom.
\end{proof}
