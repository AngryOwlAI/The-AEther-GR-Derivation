## Findings

### Finding 1: The active benchmark package is correctly classified as exact-GR rather than modified gravity.

The repository's strongest category claim survives scrutiny: the live benchmark package is not being presented as a low-energy replacement of GR, but as an exact adoption of the GR observational/dynamical package together with an interpretive ontology. Once a project states that the low-energy action is Einstein-Hilbert plus ordinary matter, that one operative metric governs null cones, proper time, and free fall, and that matter couples universally to that same operative metric, it has moved out of the category "alternative low-energy gravity theory" and into the category "exact-GR package with added interpretation."

**Manuscript / section:** overview article; flagship article; exact-closure note; dynamics manuscript; relativistic recovery manuscript  
**Severity / Type:** conceptual; category classification

### Finding 2: The congruence / flow layer belongs in standard GR kinematics, not Einstein-aether dynamics.

The use of a future-directed unit timelike congruence \(u^\mu\), together with its decomposition into expansion, shear, vorticity, and acceleration, is standard classical GR machinery. On the repository's own presentation, the congruence is not a second low-energy field equation and is generally non-unique. That matters. A chosen congruence inside an already-given metric spacetime is not the same thing as a **dynamical unit timelike vector field in the action**, which is exactly what defines Einstein-aether theory.

This is the single most important classification boundary for outside readers, because the word "Æther" will otherwise tempt them into the wrong adjacent literature category.

**Manuscript / section:** flow-geometry manuscript; foundations manuscript  
**Severity / Type:** conceptual; literature placement

### Finding 3: The contrast with Einstein-aether and Hořava gravity is materially correct, but should be stated earlier and more bluntly.

Einstein-aether theory is GR coupled to a dynamical unit timelike vector field. Hořava gravity and related preferred-foliation theories modify the symmetry structure and introduce a preferred slicing or equivalent hypersurface-orthogonal aether sector. By contrast, the benchmark package in this repository claims ordinary GR gauge structure and exactly the ordinary low-energy tensor content. On the repository's own terms, that is enough to say: **this project is not Einstein-aether theory, not Hořava gravity, and not a currently formulated preferred-frame low-energy modification of GR.**

That sentence should appear near the top of the front-facing article, not merely in supporting notes, because it is the fastest way to prevent category confusion.

**Manuscript / section:** literature-positioning note; flagship article; consistency manuscript  
**Severity / Type:** conceptual; comparison against adjacent frameworks

### Finding 4: The consistency manuscript should be framed as an audit against ADM / GR baselines, not as a new dynamical discovery.

The consistency package appears to be doing a useful job, but its role should be described carefully. Showing ordinary diffeomorphism redundancy, ordinary constraint structure, and exactly two propagating tensor polarizations is not by itself a new gravity result. It is an audit that the benchmark package really is the ordinary GR sector the repository says it is. That is a legitimate contribution in the sense of package discipline and internal coherence, but not a new low-energy theory contribution.

**Manuscript / section:** consistency manuscript; exact-closure note  
**Severity / Type:** conceptual; novelty boundary

### Finding 5: The open derivational ambition belongs to a well-populated existing literature, and Packet C is right to say so.

The repository should not claim novelty at the level of the general thought "perhaps Einstein gravity or relativistic geometry emerges from deeper structure." That general ambition is already populated by multiple traditions: thermodynamic derivations, induced gravity, canonical reconstruction, self-coupling consistency routes, Lovelock-type uniqueness arguments, and analogue-gravity lessons about the ease of obtaining effective Lorentzian geometry relative to the much harder problem of deriving the Einstein equations.

Packet C is directionally right to place the project inside that broad conversation rather than outside it.

**Manuscript / section:** literature-positioning note; project identity note; publication architecture  
**Severity / Type:** conceptual; literature saturation / novelty discipline

### Finding 6: The surviving novelty is narrow, but real.

There is still a defensible novelty claim here, but it is narrower than "new gravity theory" and narrower than "gravity may emerge from deeper microphysics." The surviving novelty lies in **research architecture and discipline**:

- exact-GR benchmark adoption first,
- derivation burden second,
- explicit benchmark gates for any acceptable deeper completion,
- refusal to blur benchmark success with derivation success,
- and public preservation of a scoped no-go result when a frozen line fails.

That is a legitimate and fairly unusual methodological stance. It should be claimed as such and not inflated into a new low-energy gravitational dynamics claim.

**Manuscript / section:** exact-closure sequence; derivation-gates note; Packet B no-go routing layer  
**Severity / Type:** conceptual; surviving novelty
