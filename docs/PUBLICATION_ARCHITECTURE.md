## Canonical Split

- Product A is the flagship benchmark package paper: the positive exact-GR theory statement already in hand.
- Product B is the frozen-line no-go paper: the bounded obstruction result on the current primitive-reservoir bridge object.
- Product B does not replace Product A.
- Product A does not quietly smuggle in Product B as if the no-go were its main scientific payload.

The publication architecture should therefore let a reader:

- accept Product A while rejecting the derivational program entirely
- read Product B as a bounded negative result without inferring that the benchmark package failed
- see that any resumed derivational work would be a genuinely new line rather than another installment of the frozen one

## Product A

### Working role

`The Æther-Flow Interpretation of Relativity` as an exact-GR benchmark and interpretive package.

### Purpose

Present the overview-first exact-closure sequence as the flagship scientific result of the repository: an exact relativistic benchmark package that adopts GR exactly at observable scale while keeping the `Æther` / `Æther-flow` vocabulary disciplined and non-overclaiming.

### Core claim

The active benchmark package gives a coherent exact-GR interpretive statement of `The Æther-Flow Interpretation of Relativity` with one operative metric and universal matter coupling, while making no claim that the current repository has already derived that benchmark from deeper substrate structure.

### Draft abstract

`The Æther-Flow Interpretation of Relativity` is presented here as an exact-GR benchmark package rather than as a low-energy modification of general relativity. The package adopts the Einsteinian observer-level law exactly, keeps one operative metric with universal matter coupling, and uses the `Æther` / `Æther-flow` ontology only as a disciplined interpretive and geometric framework for that same relativistic content. The benchmark package is organized by the exact-closure sequence: overview, exact-closure anchor, foundations, dynamics, consistency, relativistic recovery, and flow geometry. Its scientific role is positive but bounded. It states an exact relativistic theory at observable scale, clarifies its nonclaims, and separates adoption from derivation. The current frozen primitive-reservoir derivation line is therefore downstream of this package and is relevant only as a bounded no-go on a separate frozen bridge object, not as part of the benchmark claim itself.

### Claim boundary paragraph

This product claims an exact-GR benchmark package only. It adopts GR exactly at observable scale, keeps one operative metric and the ordinary matter route fixed, and uses the `Æther` / `Æther-flow` vocabulary as ontological and interpretive structure rather than as a second low-energy field law. It does not claim a completed first-principles substrate derivation, an observer-level preferred-frame modification of GR, or an independent low-energy non-GR sector. The current-line no-go is cited only to mark the status of downstream derivational work, not to define the benchmark package itself.

## Product B

### Working role

Current-line obstruction / no-go on the frozen primitive-reservoir bridge object.

### Purpose

Present the bounded negative result cleanly: the present frozen primitive-reservoir line does not derive the benchmark package because it lacks the observer-localizing structure needed to discharge the benchmark derivation gates.

### Core claim

On the fixed one-metric primitive-reservoir line now frozen by the bridge-object synthesis, derivation-attempt, and obstruction manuscripts, the preserved bridge object is observer-law silent. Gates (1) through (4) therefore fail on the current line, so the correct verdict is `Not Derived On Current Line`.

### Draft abstract

This paper records a bounded obstruction result for the current primitive-reservoir derivation line of `The Æther-Flow Interpretation of Relativity`. The benchmark target is fixed in advance by the exact-closure package and by the benchmark derivation gates: one operative metric, exact Einsteinian observer dynamics, ordinary relativistic gauge and degree-of-freedom content, exact null/proper-time/redshift recovery, and no genuine observer-side remainder. The present line keeps the same one-metric benchmark outputs and freezes a definite bridge object built from the explicit substrate equation / symmetry / constraint package together with the primitive reversible constrained dynamics family that forces it. What it does not provide is the benchmark-closing observer-localizing ingredient: no local map from substrate variables to observer spacetime fields, no local operative-metric field law, and no internal law generating curvature, null structure, proper time, or redshift from the frozen bridge object itself. On that fixed line, the result is therefore a scoped obstruction rather than a suspended open problem: the honest verdict is `Not Derived On Current Line`, and any resumed derivational program must begin on a genuinely new line with new localizing structure.

### Claim boundary paragraph

This product claims a bounded obstruction on the current frozen one-metric line only. It does not claim that no possible `Æther` / `Æther-flow` completion could ever recover the benchmark package, and it does not demote the benchmark package already fixed by adoption. Its negative result is narrower and more precise: on the present frozen bridge object, without introducing a genuinely new observer-localizing law, new primitive variable, or new symmetry principle, the benchmark derivation gates cannot be discharged.

5. Missing observer-localizing structure
6. Obstruction theorem and scoped `Not Derived On Current Line` verdict

### Product-B non-negotiables

- Begin by treating the benchmark package as already fixed, not as something Product B must recreate.
- Present the no-go as bounded and line-specific, not as a failure of the benchmark package.
- State explicitly that Product B is a negative result on a frozen bridge object, not the new flagship of the repository.

## Cross-Talk Rules

- Product A may cite Product B for current derivational status, but Product A should remain readable and scientifically complete without a long no-go narrative.
- Product B may cite Product A as the benchmark target, but Product B should not relitigate the benchmark package as if its job were to prove Product A unnecessary.
- Product A should use Product B mainly in nonclaims, status, and future-work paragraphs.
- Product B should use Product A mainly in target-definition and claim-boundary paragraphs.
- If a sentence makes Product A sound like a derivation-failure paper, move it to Product B or cut it.
- If a sentence makes Product B sound like a replacement front door for the project, cut it or rewrite it as bounded downstream status.
