# Packet A: Benchmark Package Review

Last updated: April 16, 2026

## Purpose

This packet asks a reviewer to assess the flagship exact-GR benchmark package as a positive scientific deliverable.

The review target is not whether the repository has already derived GR from substrate microphysics.
The target is whether the overview-first exact-closure package is internally coherent, mathematically disciplined, and scientifically honest about what it does and does not claim.

## Status Snapshot

- Benchmark package: fixed flagship result.
- Relation to GR: exact-GR benchmark and interpretive package.
- Nonclaim: no completed first-principles substrate derivation is being claimed here.
- Downstream status: the current frozen primitive-reservoir line is a separate bounded no-go, not the main payload of this packet.

## What The Reviewer Is Being Asked To Judge

Please focus on three primary questions:

1. Is the benchmark package internally coherent?
2. Is the benchmark package scientifically honest about its exact claim boundary?
3. Is there any defensible novelty beyond “GR plus different words,” and if so, where is it real rather than merely verbal?

## Core Claims Under Review

The package claims that:

- `The Æther-Flow Interpretation of Relativity` is the active exact relativistic theory statement of the project
- the observer-level law is GR exactly, with one operative metric and universal matter coupling
- the `Æther` / `Æther-flow` vocabulary functions as disciplined ontology and interpretation rather than as a second low-energy field law
- the benchmark package is already a positive deliverable even though a deeper substrate derivation remains open

The package does not claim that:

- GR has already been derived from substrate microphysics
- a distinct low-energy non-GR sector has been established
- the project is already a low-energy modification or replacement of GR

## Specific Review Questions

1. Does the benchmark package read as one coherent theory statement rather than as a pile of loosely related notes?
2. Are the overview, exact-closure note, and five core companion papers aligned on notation, scope, and role?
3. Is the distinction between adoption and derivation kept clear throughout?
4. Does any passage accidentally imply a new low-energy gravity theory rather than an exact-GR benchmark package?
5. Is the claim boundary narrow enough and explicit enough for skeptical readers?
6. Does the flow-geometry layer behave as an interpretive dictionary rather than as a stealth second dynamics paper?
7. Does the package state enough positive content to stand on its own even if the derivational program is rejected?
8. Where, if anywhere, is the novelty real rather than merely a change in presentation?
9. Which equation, definition, or logical transition is the weakest point in the package as written?
10. What should be cut, rewritten, or downgraded before outside circulation?

## Particularly Useful Feedback

- a place where adoption and derivation are being conflated
- a place where the package sounds like a non-GR theory even though it should not
- a place where the package sounds like “just GR with ontology” and needs either sharper honesty or sharper framing
- a definition, equation, or transition that is not rigorous enough
- a claim of novelty that should be narrowed or removed

## What Not To Spend Time On In This Packet

- Do not treat Packet A as a review of whether the current primitive-reservoir line derived GR.
- Do not spend most of the review on historical same-output relay manuscripts.
- Do not demote the benchmark package merely because the downstream derivation line is unfinished.
