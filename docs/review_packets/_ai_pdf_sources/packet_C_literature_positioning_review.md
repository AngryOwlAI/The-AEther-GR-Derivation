# Packet C: Literature And Novelty Positioning Review

Last updated: April 17, 2026

## Purpose

This packet asks a reviewer to assess whether the project is being placed honestly and conservatively in the surrounding gravity literature.

The core issue is category discipline:

- what the active benchmark package is
- what it is not
- what kind of derivational ambition remains open
- where any defensible novelty claim actually survives

## Status Snapshot

- Benchmark package: Product A is the exact-GR interpretive package at observable scale.
- Current-line result: Product B is the bounded frozen-line no-go, not a derivation success.
- Future-line status: any reopened derivational ambition would have to be a separate new-line program after the charter is completed concretely at equation level.
- Review target: literature classification and novelty discipline across that split, not the whole derivational archive and not a request to reopen the frozen line.

## What The Reviewer Is Being Asked To Judge

Please focus on three primary questions:

1. What prior work must be cited directly?
2. Is the Einstein-aether / emergent-gravity / induced-gravity / preferred-foliation positioning accurate?
3. Is any claimed novelty overstated, underspecified, or miscategorized?

## Core Claims Under Review

The packet claims that:

- the active benchmark package is closer to an exact-GR interpretive package than to a distinct low-energy alternative gravity theory
- Product A and Product B now have different literature roles: Product A is the positive exact-GR package, while Product B is the bounded frozen-line no-go and should not be treated as a failure of Product A
- any future derivational ambition belongs to a separate new-line program rather than to a continuation of Product B
- the congruence-based flow dictionary should be placed inside the existing classical GR congruence literature rather than treated as a new observer-level field theory
- the consistency discussion should be read against the ADM baseline as an analysis of the adopted GR sector
- any future reopened derivation ambition belongs to an already populated emergent-gravity / induced-gravity conversation rather than to the current frozen line itself
- the project should not be presented as Einstein-aether theory or as any current low-energy preferred-frame modification of GR
- any defensible novelty claim is narrower than “new gravity theory” and narrower than “gravity might emerge from deeper microphysics”

The packet does not claim that:

- the project has no novelty at all
- the ontology alone creates a new literature category
- Product B is still an unfinished positive derivation paper waiting only for more depth
- the open derivation program is unprecedented simply because the ontology differs

## Specific Review Questions

1. Is “Product A exact-GR interpretive package, Product B bounded frozen-line no-go, and any future reopened derivation only as a separate new-line program” the right category framing?
2. Does the public literature story keep Product A and Product B separated cleanly enough?
3. Is the package foregrounding the classical congruence / 1+3 covariant GR baseline clearly enough?
4. Is the consistency framing aligned clearly enough with the ADM canonical baseline?
5. Is the comparison against standard GR with interpretive language fair, or does it still overstate distinctiveness?
6. Is the contrast with Einstein-aether theory accurate at the level of low-energy content and extra modes?
7. Is the positioning relative to Jacobson-style or other emergent-gravity programs accurate once future derivational work is separated from the frozen current line?
8. Is the positioning relative to induced gravity and Sakharov-style emergence accurate?
9. Is the contrast with Horava gravity or other preferred-foliation alternatives stated fairly?
10. Which directly relevant literature is still missing from the current matrix, flagship article, or baseline discussion?
11. Is any claimed novelty real, and if so is it best located in ontology, package discipline, derivation architecture, negative-result discipline, or somewhere else?
12. Which novelty claims should be cut, narrowed, or made more explicit?
13. Is there any place where the project is still likely to be misread as either a low-energy alternative gravity theory or a single blurred benchmark-plus-derivation paper?

## Particularly Useful Feedback

- a missing comparison target that changes the classification
- a prior paper that should be cited directly rather than only gestured at
- a place where the project is still being described too grandly
- a place where the project is underselling a real but narrow novelty
- a better category label than the one currently used

## What Not To Spend Time On In This Packet

- Do not judge novelty by assuming the project is claiming a completed GR derivation if it is not.
- Do not treat the open derivation ambition as identical to the benchmark package already in hand.
- Do not read Packet C as a request to reopen Product B as if the frozen line were still the live derivational program.
- Do not collapse Product A and Product B back into one blurred story when assessing literature placement.
